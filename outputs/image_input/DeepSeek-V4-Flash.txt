=== What's the cheapest plant? (examples/image_plantpage.png) ===
That's a tricky question because "cheapest" can depend on whether you mean **money**, **time**, **effort**, or **space**. Here’s a breakdown of the cheapest plants from different angles:

### 1. The Absolute Cheapest (Cost in Money): Free Plants
You cannot beat **free**. The cheapest plant is one you don't pay for.
*   **Pothos & Spider Plants:** These are the kings of "plant propagation." Almost everyone who owns one is happy to give you a cutting or a "baby" (spiderette) for free. A single cutting in a glass of water will root in weeks.
*   **Succulent Leaves:** A single leaf from a succulent like a *jade plant* or *echeveria* can fall off and grow into a whole new plant.
*   **Kitchen Scraps:** You can regrow green onions, lettuce, celery, and even avocado pits from scraps you'd otherwise throw away.

### 2. The Cheapest to Buy (Money): Low-Cost, High-Volume Plants
If you *must* buy something, these are typically the cheapest at a nursery or grocery store ($2–$5).
*   **Pothos (Epipremnum aureum):** Often sold in tiny 2-inch pots for under $5. They are incredibly hardy and hard to kill.
*   **Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum):** Extremely common, very cheap, and grows like a weed.
*   **Succulents (e.g., Echeveria, Haworthia):** Small, single succulents are often the cheapest potted plants you can find.
*   **Air Plants (Tillandsia):** Sometimes a single small one can be found for $3–$5, and they need no soil.
*   **Aloe Vera:** A small aloe pup is often very cheap and incredibly useful (for burns).

### 3. The Cheapest in Terms of Total Value (Long-Term Costs)
This is the plant that gives you the **most return for the least money, time, and effort**.

**Winner: Pothos (especially the "Golden" or "Jade" variety).**
*   **Cost:** Very cheap to buy (or free).
*   **Maintenance:** Virtually impossible to kill. Thrives in low light, survives neglect, doesn't need fertilizer.
*   **Replicability:** One plant can become 100 plants over a few years via cuttings.
*   **Lifespan:** Can live for decades with minimal care.

**Runner-up: Snake Plant (Dracaena trifasciata).**
*   **Cost:** Slightly more than pothos (maybe $8–$12 for a small one), but they are **the most resilient plants on the planet**. They can survive months without water, very low light, and almost any temperature.

### 4. The Cheapest "Edible" Plant (Money & Effort)
*   **Green Onions:** Buy a bunch for $1 from the grocery store, use the green tops, put the white roots (with an inch of stem) in a glass of water, and you'll have a new plant for free.

**Final Verdict:** If you want the single **cheapest plant in terms of money** (and are willing to ask a friend), it's a **free cutting of a Pothos or Spider Plant**. If you want the **cheapest to buy right now**, it's a **small succulent** or **basic Pothos** from a big-box store.
