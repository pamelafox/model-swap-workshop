According to the sources, the California carpenter bee (*Xylocopa californica*) builds its nest as follows:

*   **Substrate and method:** It carves its nest directly into wood or into the hollow stems of plants such as Yucca and Agave. The bee digs into the wood using its sharp mandibles while vibrating its body, and it does **not** consume the wood. [[California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4]]
*   **Structure:** It first digs a tunnel and then excavates chambers to the right and left, creating a T-shaped nest. [[California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4]]
*   **Requirements:** Successful nesting depends on the availability of pollen and nectar in the area, as well as the presence of suitable nest substrate (e.g., the quantity of available stalks influences overall nest density). [[California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4]]
