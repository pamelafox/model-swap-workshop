### **California Carpenter Bees (Xylocopa californica)**
1. **Nest Construction**:
   - They **carve nests in wood** using their sharp mandibles while vibrating their bodies, but they **do not eat the wood** [California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4].
   - They dig tunnels in **live or dead wood** and hollow stems of plants like **Yucca and Agave**, creating a **T-shaped nest** (a main tunnel with side branches) [California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4].
   - Nest success depends on **available pollen/nectar** and **suitable wood substrates** [California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4].

2. **Social Structure**:
   - They are **solitary bees**, meaning each female builds and provisions her own nest [California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4].

### **Honey Bees (Apis mellifera)** *(implied from carpenter bee sources)*
1. **Nest Construction**:
   - Honey bees **do not excavate wood**—they build **wax combs** inside **pre-existing cavities** (e.g., tree hollows, beehives) [California_carpenter_bee.pdf-4 (implied by contrast)].
   - They **produce wax** from special glands to construct hexagonal cells for brood and food storage.

2. **Social Structure**:
